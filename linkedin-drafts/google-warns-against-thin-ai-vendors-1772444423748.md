# LinkedIn Draft — For us European SMBs – law firms, accountancies, consultancies – this is a critical signal. It means many of the slick AI tools you see advertised might be built on shaky ground. If a vendor's offering is simply putting a nice interface on top of GPT-5 or Gemini, without deep, proprietary value or specialized data, their long-term viability is questionable.

Many new AI tools are just a slick interface for someone else's core technology, like a fancy new casing on an old Rolodex.

Google's VP of Global Startup Organization just confirmed what top investors have whispered for a while. "LLM wrappers" – vendors simply layering on top of GPT-5 or Gemini – have shaky ground. Their long-term viability is questionable.

Think of it like buying a custom folder for your file cabinet, but the cabinet itself belongs to someone else. When the market matures, these "resellers" struggle, just like the early cloud aggregators did.

For law firms, consultancies, or any SMB, this is a critical signal. Don't commit to solutions that lack a deep, proprietary advantage. Ask: What unique data, models, or IP are you bringing, beyond a public LLM?

Demand to see their defensible moat, their core engine, not just the polished dashboard. Your long-term AI strategy requires stable, truly innovative partners.
