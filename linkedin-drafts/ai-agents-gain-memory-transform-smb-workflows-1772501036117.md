# LinkedIn Draft — Forget the developer jargon. For your law firm or consultancy, CoPaw signals a shift towards AI tools that actually remember context across client interactions and different platforms. This means future AI assistants won't just give generic answers; they'll learn your specific processes, hold long-term memory about your clients and cases, and crucially, let you control where that sensitive data resides, locally or in a private cloud.

Your AI assistant is about to get a memory. No more starting over with every interaction.

This isn't just about faster answers. Imagine a new switchboard operator who remembers every client, every past query, and your firm's specific processes. Future AI tools will learn your workflows and hold long-term context, just like a well-maintained client file cabinet.

A new framework, CoPaw, signals this shift. It means AI agents can now retain specific client and case data over time, giving you control over where that sensitive information resides.

My advice: don't try to build this yourself. Instead, spend the next six months identifying tasks like client intake or contract reviews where an AI assistant *with memory* would transform efficiency.

Executives, pinpoint your firm's most repetitive, memory-intensive operations. That's where AI agents will deliver real returns, likely for €500-€2000/month.
