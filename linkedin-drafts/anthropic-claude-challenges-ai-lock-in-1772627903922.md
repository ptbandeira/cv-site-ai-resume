# LinkedIn Draft — For European SMBs like yours, this isn't just about switching bots. It's a clear signal that the AI market is maturing and competition is heating up. What AI-natives often miss is that vendor lock-in, even with AI, is a real risk. This move gives you more power: you're less tied to a single provider, making it easier to leverage the best AI for each specific task or client, without losing your firm's accumulated context.

The AI market just signaled a challenge to vendor lock-in. Anthropic's Claude now lets you migrate chatbot history from rivals like ChatGPT. This isn't a feature; it's about owning your firm's accumulated knowledge.

Think being stuck with one proprietary switchboard operator for all calls. Moving your firm's context used to feel like shifting client files from incompatible cabinets, losing half the contents. That barrier is lower now.

This shift gives you leverage. You are less tied to a single AI provider, picking the best tool for tasks without losing your firm's hard-won context. Don't settle for the first AI.

Executives, dedicate hours next week to pilot Claude on a complex workflow. Compare its context retention directly against your current AI. Ensure you use the *most effective* AI for your business.
