# LinkedIn Draft — For you, this isn't about building complex multi-agent systems, but understanding that simply adding more AI tools or agents to a workflow doesn't automatically boost efficiency. Google's findings confirm what I've seen: more tools can create overhead, and the 'best' AI setup depends entirely on the specific task, especially for document-heavy work. There's a point of diminishing returns.

Stacking AI tools like a tower of fax machines won't make your office more productive.

Google's research confirms what we've seen: adding more agents can create more overhead than value. It's like having five switchboard operators for a single incoming call.

Forget the shiny new objects. Pinpoint one critical task – contract review, financial reports. Pilot one AI solution there. Measure its real impact before you even think about expanding. This isn't about collecting tools for your digital file cabinet.

For my clients, we spend four weeks defining scope and proving initial ROI. This focused approach cuts through the noise and delivers tangible results.

Executives: Treat AI like a precision instrument, not a blunt object. Focus on deep impact in one area first.
