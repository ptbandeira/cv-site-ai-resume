# LinkedIn Draft — For your European SMB, this isn't just tech news; it's a stark reminder about AI vendor trust and data sovereignty. When you entrust your firm's data to an AI provider, you're not just buying a tool, you're buying into their values and their data handling policies. This event underscores the critical importance of understanding where your sensitive information resides and who has access to it, especially when geopolitical factors come into play.

Your firm's data in an AI tool isn't just code; it's on a server, potentially a government server.

OpenAI opened its digital file cabinet to the Pentagon, shedding 1.5 million users in days. Anthropic, keeping its data private, saw its Rolodex expand.

This isn't just about a software update. It's about who gets to dial into your sensitive client information and what values guide your chosen AI switchboard operator.

Know your data export strategy now, like you'd know how to unplug a critical server. And start evaluating alternative AI vendors before your current one routes your data through a foreign switchboard.

Executive takeaway: Implement a robust data export plan for every AI tool you use, today.
