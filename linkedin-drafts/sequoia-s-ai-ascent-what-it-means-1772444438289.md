# LinkedIn Draft — Forget the "biggest opportunity" hype. What matters for your law firm or consultancy is Sequoia's insistence that value is in the *application layer*, not the underlying models. This means the powerful AI tools you *use* will drive your competitive edge, not complex internal AI development. The shift to an "Agent Economy" isn't about sci-fi; it's about highly autonomous AI tools giving lean teams the leverage of much larger ones, fundamentally changing how work gets done.

The competitive edge in AI isn't about building your own supercomputer. It's about how you use the software.

Think of it like this: you don't need to engineer a new telephone switchboard from scratch. You just need to connect to the best service and let it handle the calls. Value sits in the application layer, not the underlying models.

This shift to an "Agent Economy" means highly autonomous AI tools giving lean teams the leverage of much larger ones. Imagine a Rolodex that not only stores contacts but schedules meetings and drafts follow-ups.

For your law firm or consultancy, this isn't science fiction. Start with off-the-shelf AI agents on specific, repetitive tasks. Pick one workflow, like initial document drafting or data synthesis. Allocate a small team to manage and debug these new systems.

The takeaway for executives is clear: stop planning for the distant future. Begin deploying practical AI agents today.
