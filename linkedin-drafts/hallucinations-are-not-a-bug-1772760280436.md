# LinkedIn Draft — For your business, this isn't a headline promising an immediate fix. What it tells me, based on years in this field, is that hallucinations are not a bug to be patched but an inherent characteristic. This deeper understanding of *how* LLMs hallucinate doesn't change the immediate reality: AI will continue to generate convincing falsehoods, and you must operate under that assumption.

Imagine your fax machine occasionally fabricating entire paragraphs. That's the reality of AI hallucinations. This isn't a glitch; it's how these models are fundamentally built.

Researchers found specific "H-Neurons" – less than 0.1% of the system – that reliably cause these fabrications. It's an internal design choice, not an external error you patch.

Forget waiting for a "cure" for these H-Neurons. Your priority is rigorous human oversight. Start piloting AI on low-risk internal tasks: drafting summaries, initial research.

For any client-facing output, a human must review and fact-check, without exception. This isn't optional; it's the cost of reliable AI integration. Embed these checks into your workflows now.
