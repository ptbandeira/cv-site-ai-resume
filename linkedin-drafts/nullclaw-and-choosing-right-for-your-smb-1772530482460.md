# LinkedIn Draft — While NullClaw's extreme efficiency is impressive for developers building highly specialized, resource-constrained AI applications, it's not a direct tool for your business today. For law firms, accountancies, or consultancies, this signals a future where AI components could run faster and cheaper *within* existing software, rather than requiring dedicated, heavy infrastructure.

The latest AI framework, NullClaw, runs on 1MB of RAM. That is less space than a single document on your old 1990s file server.

This isn't a new fax machine for your office today. It signals a future where AI components plug into existing software like a new module in your old PABX switchboard, running faster and cheaper within your current systems.

My advice remains consistent: ignore the noise around every new agent framework. Chasing the latest shiny object is like constantly reorganizing your physical file cabinets instead of getting work done.

Instead, identify specific, high-value problems within your operations that AI can genuinely solve. You don't need a new Rolodex when you need a better sales process.

For executives, focus on the business problem, not the AI’s technical footprint.
